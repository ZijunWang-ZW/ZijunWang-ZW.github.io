Introduction

MyFRIEND is an international initiative to provide guidance for the use of guidelines in multimorbidity related practice. The MyFRIEND working group was established in 2023, and consists of more than 30 experts in multimorbidity research and guideline development from 13 countries across the world.

The MyFRIEND framework is a user-friendly tool that helps clinicians and other health professionals to select and use guidelines and recommendations, both for the individual comorbidities and for multimorbidity itself. The tool is currently being finalized and will be released on this website during the course of the year 2026. Meanwhile, please have a look at our previous studies:

  • Study protocol: https://www.researchprotocols.org/2025/1/e63339
  • Systematic review of health-centered multimorbidity guidelines: https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S1568-1637(24)00377-5
  • Global survey of frontline healthcare workers on the use of guidelines in multimorbidity practice: https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0895-4356(25)00448-2
  • Systematic review of guidelines on diabetes, heart disease and stroke: https://doi.org/10.1111/jep.70385
  • Series of interviews of multimorbidity experts – to be published soon

MyFRIEND is endorsed by the Respiratory Effectiveness Group (REG, www.regresearchnetwork.org), and we work in close collaboration with the Guidelines International Network (GIN, www.g-i-n.net) Multimorbidity Working Group.

We believe that MyFRIEND – once released – will be of great help for healthcare professionals to find the optimal recommendations for guiding the care of multimorbid patients.

We are also looking for more partners across the world to help to implement and test our framework in practice. If you would like to join our working group, and/or adapt or translate the framework to your setting, or have any questions or feedback – please feel free to contact us!
